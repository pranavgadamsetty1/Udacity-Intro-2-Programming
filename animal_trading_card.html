Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is the largest living cat species. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange-brown fur with a lighter underside.

  • Scientific name: Panthera Tigris Tigris
  • Length: 2.4m - 3.3m
  • Lifespan: 18 - 25 years
  • Habitat: Dense tropical forest and mangroves

The tiger is an apex predator, primarily preying on ungulates such as deer and wild boar. It is territorial and generally a solitary but social predator, requiring large contiguous areas of habitat, which supports its requirements for prey and rearing of its offspring.
